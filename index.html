Grade 9

Building Academic Foundations

After skipping 8th grade, I joined a new class of previous seniors. I focused mostly on studying for the entrance exam and worked on my math and language skills outside of school. After graduating from a local junior high school with 35.8/36 on the entrance exam, instead of going to Taipei First Girls' High School, I selected an unconventional path: transferring to international education.

Grade 10

Deepening Exploration

I adapted to the new bilingual environment and coursework, and my curiosity thrived. Although falling behind on English speaking at first, I caught up thanks to debate and business presentation experiences. In addition, I started self-studying economics through Khan Academy, and later joined related competitions. This opened my econ/business academic path.

Grade 11

Connecting Knowledge to Direction

I expanded on clubs, volunteering, and started new passion projects. I challenged myself through AP courses, standardized tests, economics competitions, and independent research. I became increasingly interested in using economic reasoning and empirical methods to analyze social issues. Also, I began connecting my academic interests with future goals in economics, finance, and research. My learning became less about collecting certificates and more about developing a clearer intellectual direction.

What This Journey Means to Me

From Achievement to Inquiry

During high school, I gradually learned that academic growth is not only about completing tasks or collecting achievements. Research, debate, competitions, and self-study taught me that meaningful learning begins with asking better questions.

My interests may seem broad at first: economics, finance, mathematics, language, writing, and public speaking. However, they are connected by one central goal: I want to use analysis and communication to understand the world more deeply.
